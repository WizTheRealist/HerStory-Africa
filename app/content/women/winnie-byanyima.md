## Biography

Winnie Byanyima was born on 13 January 1959 in Mbarara, Uganda. She studied aeronautical engineering at the University of Manchester, becoming one of very few African women to qualify in that field. She later earned a master's degree in mechanical engineering from Cranfield University.

She returned to Uganda and became involved in the struggle against the Idi Amin and Milton Obote dictatorships. She joined the National Resistance Movement and was part of the bush war that brought Yoweri Museveni to power in 1986. She was later elected to the Ugandan Parliament, where she served for 11 years and became a fierce advocate for women's rights.

She moved into international development, becoming Director of Gender and Development at the United Nations Development Programme. She then served as Executive Director of Oxfam International from 2013 to 2019. In 2019, she was appointed Executive Director of UNAIDS, the Joint United Nations Programme on HIV/AIDS.

## What She Fought For

Byanyima has spent her career making the connection between inequality and suffering. At Oxfam, she became one of the world's leading voices on extreme wealth inequality, drawing global attention to the gap between the richest people in the world and everyone else. She pushed the issue onto the agenda of the World Economic Forum in Davos every year, releasing reports showing how the world's billionaires hold more wealth than billions of ordinary people combined.

At UNAIDS, she leads the global effort to end the HIV epidemic by 2030. She has been outspoken about the way that gender inequality, poverty, and stigma fuel the spread of HIV, particularly among young women in sub-Saharan Africa. She has called out pharmaceutical companies for keeping life-saving medication out of reach of poor countries and has pushed hard for vaccine equity during the COVID-19 pandemic.

## Major Achievements

- Elected to the Ugandan Parliament and served for 11 years
- Director of Gender and Development at UNDP
- Executive Director of Oxfam International (2013 to 2019)
- Executive Director of UNAIDS (2019 to present)
- One of the leading global voices on wealth inequality and corporate accountability
- Consistently named among the most powerful women in international affairs

## Her Impact Today

Winnie Byanyima sits at the intersection of global health, gender rights, and economic justice. Her work at UNAIDS makes her responsible for one of the most important public health campaigns in human history. She approaches that work not just as a technical problem but as a question of power, asking who benefits and who gets left behind, and she is not quiet about the answers.
