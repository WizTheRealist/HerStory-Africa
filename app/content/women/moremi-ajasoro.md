## Biography

Moremi Ajasoro is a legendary figure from the history of Ile-Ife, the ancient city considered the cradle of Yoruba civilisation in what is now Osun State, Nigeria. The exact dates of her life are uncertain but oral tradition places her in the 12th century CE.

According to Yoruba oral history, the people of Ife were being repeatedly raided by a neighbouring group known as the Igbo (a separate people from the present-day Igbo of southeastern Nigeria). These raiders would attack Ife, capture people and carry them away. The raids were devastating and the people of Ife did not know how to stop them.

Moremi, a woman of great beauty and bravery, made a decision. She would allow herself to be captured in the next raid so she could infiltrate the enemy's community, learn their secrets, and bring the information back to Ife.

Before going, she visited the Esimirin River and made a vow to the river deity: if she returned safely and her mission succeeded, she would give whatever the deity asked of her in return.

She was captured as planned. Because of her beauty and bearing, she was taken as a wife by the enemy king. She learned the secrets of their warriors, including the fact that their frightening appearance during raids came from costumes made of grass. Once she had the knowledge she needed, she escaped and made her way back to Ife.

She told the people of Ife what she had learned. Armed with that knowledge, they used fire against the grass costumes in battle. The raids stopped.

Moremi returned to the Esimirin River to fulfil her vow. The deity demanded the life of her only son, Oluorogbo. She gave him. The sacrifice broke her heart, but she kept her word.

## What She Represents

Moremi is remembered as the woman who saved Ife when the men could not find a way. She used intelligence and courage rather than weapons. She sacrificed her own safety, her time, and ultimately her son to protect her community.

Her story carries a message that has resonated for centuries: that a woman's contribution to her people can be the most profound and costly contribution of all. She is celebrated in Ife every year at the Edi Festival, a tradition that has continued for centuries and is recognised by UNESCO as an important cultural heritage.

## Major Achievements

- Legendary saviour of Ile-Ife through intelligence, courage and sacrifice
- Subject of the Edi Festival in Ile-Ife, celebrated annually for nearly a thousand years
- UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage recognition for the Edi Festival
- Immortalised in a prominent statue in Ile-Ife
- A central figure in Yoruba oral tradition, literature and cultural identity
- Her story has been adapted into plays, novels and educational materials across Nigeria

## Her Impact Today

Moremi Ajasoro is one of the oldest and most enduring stories of female heroism in Nigerian history. She is taught to children in Yoruba culture as an example of sacrifice, love for community and courage. Her statue in Ile-Ife stands as a permanent reminder that women have always been at the centre of Nigerian history, not at the edges.
