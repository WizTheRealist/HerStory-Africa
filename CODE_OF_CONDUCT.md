# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

HerStory Africa documents real history — women who fought against injustice, often at great personal cost. Contributors to this project are expected to approach the work with the same seriousness and respect.

**Expected behaviour:**

- Treat all contributors, subjects, and readers with respect
- Engage with historical facts, not personal opinions about gender, feminism, or politics
- Accept corrections gracefully — accuracy matters more than being right
- Credit sources honestly
- Be welcoming to new contributors, especially those contributing from regions underrepresented in the archive

**Unacceptable behaviour:**

- Submitting deliberately false or misleading historical content
- Harassment, insults, or dismissive language toward contributors
- Trolling, bad-faith arguments, or derailing discussions
- Submitting content that diminishes, mocks, or sexualises the women documented in the archive
- Plagiarism or submitting content without proper attribution

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces: issues, pull requests, discussions, and any communication related to HerStory Africa.

## Enforcement

Violations can be reported by opening an issue or contacting the maintainer directly. Reports will be reviewed and may result in comments being removed, PRs being closed, or contributors being blocked.

This is a project about women who fought for dignity. The bar for how we treat each other here is not negotiable.
